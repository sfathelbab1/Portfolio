UX Design - Introduction to Human Computer Interaction

CollegeVibes - a sesmter long project for my HCI course, is an app aimed to best assist college students in their new and sometimes overwhelming endeavors, of making friends and long lasting relationships.

  • Interview Stage

    • For the Interview Stage, our team and I interviewed three students at Montclair State University regarding how they go about making friends at college. After finding a few patterns from the series of questions we had asked, we began to tailor their responses to satisfying their needs through our future application.

  • Target Demographic - Persona #1
  • Target Demographic - Persona #2
  • Target Demographic - Persona #3

    • Target Demographic and Persona Stage - Upon deciding what our target demographic would be, we came to the conclusion of choosing non-commuter, non-transfer, Freshman students at Montclair State University. After doing so, we tailored our three personas to that target demographic.

  • Raw Materials

    • The Raw Materials consists of features we gathered from five applications on the market. From each applications, we had selected the 'Big Idea' that would contribute to setting our own features of our application. We listed the pros and cons of each, and tailored it to fit the needs of the main features of our application.

  • Conveying the Solution

    • In Conveying Our Solution, we worked specifically on Persona #1 and showcased a scenario in which our user used our application to go about making a new friend at Montclair State University, and how the main features contributed.

  • Testing and Refining our Solution - Featuring the Final Product of the Main Feature

    • In Testing and Refining our Solution, we used the program Axure to create the final result of CollegeVibes and showcasing the unique features.

  • Usability Test [YouTube Video]

    Live Usability Testing with a fellow student testing the main features. In the video above, we test the main features of the CollegeVibes application and how the student interacted with the application as a whole while using the Think Aloud Protocol.
